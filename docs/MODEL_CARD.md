## Intended Use

### Primary Use Cases
- **Decision support** for radiologists reviewing chest X-rays
- **Screening aid** for dermatologists reviewing dermoscopy images
- **Research** on medical image AI in resource-limited settings

### Out-of-Scope Uses
- **Not for clinical diagnosis** — must be reviewed by a qualified clinician
- Not intended for autonomous, unreviewed clinical deployment
- Not validated for paediatric imaging
- Not validated for non-standard imaging protocols or equipment

---

## Training Data

| Model | Dataset | Size | Classes |
|---|---|---|---|
| DenseNet-121 | CheXpert (Stanford) | ~224,000 X-rays | 14 |
| EfficientNet-B4 | ISIC 2020 | ~33,000 images | 8 |

### Data Handling
- CheXpert uncertain labels (-1) mapped to 0 (negative)
- Frontal-only X-rays used for training
- ISIC images balanced with weighted sampling
- 80/20 train/validation split (official splits used where provided)

## Limitations and Biases

- Models trained on datasets from specific institutions may not generalise to all imaging equipment, patient demographics, or clinical workflows.
- CheXpert labels were generated by an NLP labeller from radiology reports, which introduces label noise.
- ISIC dataset over-represents lighter skin tones; performance on darker skin tones may be lower.
- Models have not been evaluated in prospective clinical studies.

---

## Ethical Considerations

This tool is developed as **decision support** software and is explicitly positioned as a second opinion, not a replacement for clinical expertise. Users must:

1. Always review predictions with a qualified clinician before any clinical decision.
2. Not deploy this tool as the sole decision-making system.
3. Monitor for performance drift across different patient populations.
